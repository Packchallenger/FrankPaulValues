FrankPaulValues

What is FrankPaulValues?

FrankPaulValues is a political quiz inspired by 8values, but tailored to the political climate of the United States Political Simulator which is a discord server. FrankPaulValues tries to be a more complete indicator of what politics people follow. This is paticularly helpful for new members which don't know what party to join.

What are the Frank Paul Values?

There are six independent axes - Economics, Perspective, Government, Welfare, Drugs, and Compassion - and each has two opposing values assigned to them. They are:

Planned (Economics)
Those with higher Planned scores believe the economy should be planned by the government to reduce exploitation from corporations.

Free Market (Economics)
Those with higher Free Market scores believe that corporations are the backbone of the economy.

Progressive (Perspective)
Those with higher Progressive scores often follow values which tend to be much more accepting of communities such as the LGBTQ community and often encourage the BLM movement.

Conservative (Perspective)
Those with higher Conservative scores belive in values which tend to be more traditional such as the nuclear family and marriage.

Safety (Government)
Those with higher Safety scores believe that safety is more important than being free, they often say you cannot be free if you are not safe.

Liberty (Government)
Those with higher Liberty scores believe that liberty is the foundation of ethics and thus you are not safe if you are not free.

Expand (Welfare)
Those with higher Expand scores believe the government should spend more money on government programs which often require high taxation.

Reduce (Welfare)
Those with higher Reduce scores believe the government should lower taxes and usually oppose welfare.

Emphathetic (Drugs)
Those with higher Emphathetic scores believe that drug consumption is a cause of poverty and are kinder to addicts.

Condemn (Drugs)
Those with higher Condemn scores believe that drugs are bad, they oppose legalization of drugs and look down upon addicts.

Compassionate (Compassion)
Those with higher Compassionate scores often want to do more to make life less hard.

Iron Fist (Compassion)
Those with higher Iron Fist scores believe that poverty should simply be more escapble instead of less hard.

What's the "Closest Match" mean at the bottom of the results?

In addition to matching you to the twelve Frank Paul Values, the quiz also attempts to match you to a political ideology. Don't take it too seriously.
